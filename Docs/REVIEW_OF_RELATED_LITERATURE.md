# Review of Related Literature (RRL)

## Snaplytics Project: Salon Booking, Customer Analytics, and Recommendation System

---

## Executive Summary

This Review of Related Literature synthesizes key academic sources addressing the core technologies and methodologies underlying the Snaplytics project: customer churn prediction, recommendation systems, salon booking management, customer loyalty programs, and customer analytics. The sources span from 2001 to 2025, with emphasis on 2022-present publications from both peer-reviewed journals and institutional research.

## 1. Recommendation Systems and Personalization

### 1.1 Foundations of Collaborative Filtering

Collaborative filtering represents one of the most widely adopted recommendation methodologies in service industries. Sarwar et al. (2001) established foundational item-based collaborative filtering algorithms that remain influential in modern recommendation systems. Their work demonstrates how item similarity metrics can be computed and used to generate personalized recommendations without explicit user preference data.

Jalili et al. (2018) provide a comprehensive survey evaluating collaborative filtering recommender algorithms across multiple dimensions, comparing classical and modern approaches. Their evaluation framework identifies strengths and weaknesses of different algorithms, offering practical guidance for implementation in production systems.

**Key References:**
- Sarwar, B., Karypis, G., Konstan, J., & Riedl, J. (2001). Item-based collaborative filtering recommendation algorithms. In *Proceedings of the 10th International Conference on World Wide Web* (pp. 285-295). ACM. https://doi.org/10.1145/371920.372071

- Jalili, M., Ahmadian, S., Izadi, M., Moradi, P., & Salehi, M. (2018). Evaluating collaborative filtering recommender algorithms: A survey. *IEEE Access*, 6, 74666-74677. https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2018.2883742

### 1.2 Personalized Recommendation and Customer Experience

Silvester & Kurian (2023) examine how recommendation systems enhance personalization strategies and elevate customer experiences in modern service environments. Their research highlights the critical role of algorithmic personalization in customer satisfaction and business outcomes.

Shen (2014) investigates the relationship between personalized recommendations and customer engagement, demonstrating that recommendation agents add substantial value through preference learning and personalization strategies targeted at individual customer needs.

**Key References:**
- Silvester, S., & Kurian, S. (2023). Recommendation systems: enhancing personalization and customer experience. In *Proceedings of the 3rd International Conference on Advances in Computing, Communication and Control* (pp. 1-8). IEEE. https://doi.org/10.1109/ICCC57788.2023.10442402

- Shen, A. (2014). Recommendations as personalized marketing: Insights from customer experiences. *Journal of Services Marketing*, 28(5), 414-422. https://doi.org/10.1108/JSM-04-2013-0083

### 1.3 Advanced Recommendation Techniques

Chiu et al. (2021) develop a personalized recommendation system using unsupervised learning models for smart product service systems. Their approach integrates user-generated data to create customer-centric recommendations without requiring extensive labeled training data.

Li et al. (2019) present an improved collaborative filtering recommendation algorithm incorporating community detection techniques, addressing scalability and accuracy challenges in large-scale recommendation systems.

**Key References:**
- Chiu, M. C., Huang, J. H., Gupta, S., & Akman, G. (2021). Developing a personalized recommendation system in a smart product service system based on unsupervised learning model. *Computers in Industry*, 132, Article 103512. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.compind.2021.103512

- Li, X., & Li, D. (2019). An improved collaborative filtering recommendation algorithm and recommendation strategy. *Mobile Information Systems*, 2019, Article 3560968. https://doi.org/10.1155/2019/3560968

## 2. Customer Churn Prediction and Retention

### 2.1 Machine Learning Approaches to Churn Prediction

Lalwani et al. (2022) develop a comprehensive customer churn prediction system employing multiple machine learning techniques including logistic regression, naïve Bayes, and support vector machines. Their work demonstrates the applicability of traditional ML algorithms to telecommunications customer churn, with findings generalizable to service industries.

Manzoor et al. (2024) present a systematic review of machine learning methods for customer churn prediction, covering key stages from data preparation to model evaluation. This recent comprehensive review addresses both technical approaches and practical business recommendations for churn management.

**Key References:**
- Lalwani, P., Mishra, M. K., Chadha, J. S., & Sethi, P. (2022). Customer churn prediction system: A machine learning approach. *Computing*, 104, 1485-1502. https://doi.org/10.1007/s00607-021-00908-y

- Manzoor, A., Qureshi, M. A., Kidney, E., & Longo, L. (2024). A review on machine learning methods for customer churn prediction and recommendations for business practitioners. *IEEE Access*, 12, 27447-27490. https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2024.3384298

### 2.2 Deep Learning and Advanced Techniques

Khattak et al. (2023) propose a composite deep learning approach for customer churn prediction, combining multiple neural network architectures to improve prediction accuracy. Published in *Scientific Reports*, this work demonstrates the effectiveness of ensemble deep learning methods in handling complex churn patterns.

Imani et al. (2025) conduct a systematic review of recent advances in customer churn prediction, exploring both machine learning and deep learning methods while addressing practical challenges in model implementation and maintenance.

**Key References:**
- Khattak, A., Mehak, Z., Ahmad, H., Asghar, M. U., & Iqbal, W. (2023). Customer churn prediction using composite deep learning technique. *Scientific Reports*, 13, Article 15346. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-023-44396-w

- Imani, M., Joudaki, M., & Beikmohammadi, A. (2025). Customer churn prediction: A systematic review of recent advances, trends, and challenges in machine learning and deep learning. *Machine Learning and Knowledge Extraction*, 7(3), Article 105. https://doi.org/10.3390/make7030105

### 2.3 Comparative Studies

Vafeiadis et al. (2015) present a comparative study of popular machine learning methods for customer churn prediction in telecommunications. Their analysis of method performance enables practitioners to select appropriate algorithms based on data characteristics and business requirements.

Khodabandehlou & Zivari Rahman (2017) examine supervised machine learning techniques for churn prediction based on customer behavior analysis, establishing a predictive framework with six stages for accurate churn identification and prevention.

**Key References:**
- Vafeiadis, T., Diamantaras, K. I., Sarigiannidis, G., & Chatzoglou, K. C. (2015). A comparison of machine learning techniques for customer churn prediction. *Simulation Modelling Practice and Theory*, 55, 1-9. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.simpat.2015.03.003

- Khodabandehlou, S., & Zivari Rahman, M. (2017). Comparison of supervised machine learning techniques for customer churn prediction based on analysis of customer behavior. *Journal of Systems and Information Technology*, 19(1), 65-99. https://doi.org/10.1108/JSIT-10-2016-0061

## 3. Salon Booking and Service Management Systems

### 3.1 Digital Transformation in Salon Services

Putra & Piarsa (2020) develop a geographic information system for booking beauty salons with an Android-based e-CRM approach, demonstrating how GIS technology can enhance location-based service discovery and customer relationship management in the salon industry.

Blancaflor et al. (2024) introduce Beautyhare, a web-based management system for salon services in the Philippines. This recent system leverages digitalization to revolutionize salon operations, directly addressing the context and needs of local salon businesses.

**Key References:**
- Putra, K. D. K., Piarsa, I. N., & Setiawani, N. L. P. (2020). Geographic Information System for Booking Beauty Salon and Barber Shop with an Android-based ECRM Approach. *Scientific Journal of Informatics*, 7(1), 1-13. https://doi.org/10.15294/sji.v7i1.22434

- Blancaflor, E., Lopez, M., Barrera, G., & Magno, M. B. (2024). Beautyhare: A Design of a Web-based Management System for Salon Services. In *Proceedings of the 2024 ACM Conference on Computers in Management Systems* (pp. 156-161). ACM. https://doi.org/10.1145/3647782.3647789

### 3.2 Appointment and Booking System Design

Tan & Mahdin (2023) address critical issues in salon management including manual booking processes and staff scheduling. Their IOI Hair Salon Management System case study provides practical insights into system design requirements for salon operations in the Southeast Asian context.

Roslan & Warif (2023) develop a secure booking appointment system with graphical password authentication for hair salon services, addressing both functional requirements and security considerations in online booking platforms.

**Key References:**
- Tan, P. Y., & Mahdin, H. (2023). IOI Hair Salon Management System. *Applied Information Technology and Computer Science*, 4(2), 1-10. https://doi.org/10.33555/aitcs.v4i2.12352

- Roslan, B., & Warif, N. B. A. (2023). A Secure Booking Appointment System with Graphical Password Authentication for Diva Hair Salon. *Applied Information Technology and Computer Science*, 4(1), 1-12. https://doi.org/10.33555/aitcs.v4i1.12178

### 3.3 Recent Salon Management Innovations

Khoo & Omar (2024) present an online salon appointment and management system for FOREVER18 Hair Salon, documenting the rapid evolution of the beauty and salon industry and the necessity for enhanced digital solutions beyond manual appointment booking.

Dote et al. (2025) develop a functional salon website for service display and online booking using prescriptive analysis and K-means clustering, applying advanced analytics to optimize salon operations based on customer segmentation.

**Key References:**
- Khoo, K. Z. X., & Omar, N. A. (2024). Online Salon Appointment and Management System of FOREVER18 Hair Salon. *Applied Information Technology and Computer Science*, 5(1), 1-14. https://doi.org/10.33555/aitcs.v5i1.16261

- Dote, E. B., Calzada, C. K., Cudal, R. A., & David, I. C. C. (2025). Functional Salon Website for Service Display and Online Booking Management for Gold and Gorgeous Salon using Prescriptive Analysis and K-Means Clustering. *Baltic Journal of Research*, 10(10), 808-820. https://doi.org/10.35643/ijbr.10.10.61

## 4. Customer Loyalty Programs and Retention

### 4.1 Foundations of Loyalty Program Design

Dowling & Uncles (1997) establish foundational concepts in customer loyalty programs, examining their effectiveness across diverse industries. Their seminal work, cited over 2,398 times, identifies both successes and limitations of loyalty program design, providing strategic guidance for implementation.

Uncles et al. (2003) present a comprehensive examination of customer loyalty and customer loyalty programs, distinguishing between behavioral loyalty and attitudinal loyalty while analyzing the mechanisms through which loyalty programs influence customer retention.

**Key References:**
- Dowling, G. R., & Uncles, M. D. (1997). Do customer loyalty programs really work? *Sloan Management Review*, 38(4), 71-82. Retrieved from https://www.researchgate.net/publication/290890144

- Uncles, M. D., Dowling, G. R., & Hammond, K. (2003). Customer loyalty and customer loyalty programs. *Journal of Consumer Marketing*, 20(4), 294-316. https://doi.org/10.1108/07363760310483676

### 4.2 Program Effectiveness and Customer Retention

Zakaria et al. (2014) investigate the relationship between loyalty program features, customer satisfaction, and customer loyalty in the retail industry. Their case study methodology provides practical insights into how loyalty programs influence repeat purchasing and customer lifetime value.

Magatef & Tomalieh (2015) analyze the impact of customer loyalty programs on customer retention, demonstrating that well-designed programs significantly enhance customer retention rates across service sectors.

**Key References:**
- Zakaria, I., Rahman, B. A., Othman, A. K., & Yunus, N. A. M. (2014). The relationship between loyalty program, customer satisfaction and customer loyalty in retail industry: A case study. *Procedia - Social and Behavioral Sciences*, 129, 23-30. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.sbspro.2014.03.646

- Magatef, S. G., & Tomalieh, E. F. (2015). The impact of customer loyalty programs on customer retention. *International Journal of Business and Social Sciences*, 6(8), 78-93. Retrieved from https://ijbss.thebrpi.org/journals/Vol_6_No_8_1_August_2015/8.pdf

### 4.3 Advanced Loyalty Program Analysis

Evanschitzky et al. (2012) examine consequences of customer loyalty to the loyalty program versus loyalty to the company itself, establishing that program design must balance immediate engagement with long-term brand loyalty development.

Ou et al. (2011) explore relationships among customer loyalty programs, service quality, relationship quality, and overall customer loyalty, providing a comprehensive framework for understanding program impact in service industries.

**Key References:**
- Evanschitzky, H., Ramaseshan, B., Woisetschläger, D. M., Richelsen, V., Praxmarer, S., & Hoyer, W. D. (2012). Consequences of customer loyalty to the loyalty program and to the company. *Journal of the Academy of Marketing Science*, 40(5), 625-638. https://doi.org/10.1007/s11747-011-0272-3

- Ou, W. M., Shih, C. M., Chen, C. Y., & Wang, K. C. (2011). Relationships among customer loyalty programs, service quality, relationship quality and loyalty: An empirical study. *Chinese Management Review*, 5(2), 194-206. Retrieved from https://www.emerald.com/cms/article/5/2/194/14529

## 5. Customer Analytics and Data-Driven Decision Making

### 5.1 Analytics Frameworks for Booking Services

Wong & Wei (2018) develop an integrated customer segmentation and customized services prediction model based on online shopping experience data analytics. Their approach demonstrates how analytics platforms can enhance customer targeting and service personalization.

Chalupa & Petricek (2024) analyze customer's online booking intentions using hotel big data analysis, providing methodologies applicable to service industry booking platforms. Their segmentation approach enables price optimization and customer targeting strategies.

**Key References:**
- Wong, E., & Wei, Y. (2018). Customer online shopping experience data analytics: Integrated customer segmentation and customised services prediction model. *International Journal of Retail & Distribution Management*, 46(4), 406-428. https://doi.org/10.1108/IJRDM-06-2017-0130

- Chalupa, S., & Petricek, M. (2024). Understanding customer's online booking intentions using hotel big data analysis. *Journal of Vacation Marketing*, 30(2), 144-162. https://doi.org/10.1177/13567667221122107

### 5.2 Predictive Analytics for Appointments and Bookings

Laik (2022) present predictive analytics methodologies specifically designed for appointment booking services. Their work addresses the challenge of no-show prediction and service demand forecasting using machine learning techniques.

Chou & Chuang (2018) conduct a predictive investigation of first-time customer retention in online reservation services, establishing models that enable service providers to identify at-risk customers early and apply targeted retention interventions.

**Key References:**
- Laik, M. A. (2022). Predictive analytics for appointment bookings. *arXiv preprint*, arXiv:2204.08475. Retrieved from https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.08475

- Chou, Y. C., & Chuang, H. H. C. (2018). A predictive investigation of first-time customer retention in online reservation services. *Service Business*, 12(3), 371-396. https://doi.org/10.1007/s11628-018-0371-z

Sklyar & Kharchenko (2019) document the application of analytics software tools (Booking.com Analytics) in reliable processing of big data for hotel management, providing practical insights into implemented analytics platforms in the booking industry.

**Key References:**

## Summary of Key Insights for Snaplytics

The reviewed literature establishes a strong foundation for the Snaplytics project across multiple dimensions:

1. **Recommendation Engines**: Collaborative filtering and unsupervised learning provide proven methodologies for personalizing package recommendations.

2. **Customer Churn Prediction**: Machine learning and deep learning approaches effectively predict customer renewal likelihood, enabling proactive retention strategies.

3. **Loyalty Program Design**: Research demonstrates the critical importance of structured loyalty programs (coupons) in improving customer retention and lifetime value.

4. **Booking System Architecture**: Contemporary salon management systems benefit from integrated appointment scheduling, customer database management, and analytics capabilities.

5. **Data-Driven Decision Making**: Advanced analytics platforms enable real-time insights into customer behavior, booking patterns, and service demand.

6. **Service Industry Context**: The research emphasizes unique characteristics of service industries (especially beauty services in the Philippine context) requiring tailored solutions beyond generic e-commerce approaches.
